SkySuite — Pre-Launch Landing Page Design Brief
Build a pre-launch marketing landing page for SkySuite, a premium aviation operations platform for flight schools, flight clubs, and aircraft partnerships. The goal is to drive early access sign-ups. This is not a legacy FBO app with a new skin — it should feel like the Apple of aviation software. Think Stripe, Linear, or Vercel energy applied to flight operations.

BRAND IDENTITY
Tagline: "Your Co-Pilot for Smarter Operations"
Colors: Sky Blue #07BCE7 · Navy #022C55 · Bright Blue #0941C4 · Sage #C2C1A5 · Cream #F5F9E9
Brand gradient (CTAs, hero accents): linear-gradient(135deg, #07BCE7, #0941C4)
Typography: Montserrat SemiBold/Bold for all headings · Inter Regular for body text
Visual language: Liquid glass — frosted card surfaces, backdrop blur, soft borders. Generous negative space. Confident typography. Subtle depth without clutter. The aesthetic should feel like premium fintech or a top-tier developer tool — not clipart cockpits and stock photos of Cessnas.

DESIGN DIRECTION
Luxury modern SaaS. The reference points are Linear.app, Stripe, Vercel, and Raycast — not FlightAware or legacy aviation portals. Specific direction:
Dark hero section anchored in deep navy (#022C55) with the brand gradient used sparingly as a luminous accent — glowing edges, gradient text on the headline, lit UI elements. Not a flat dark background. It should feel like depth.
Glass cards float over the background with visible layering — light cards on dark sections, dark cards on light sections. Real contrast, real hierarchy.
The dashboard mockup in the hero should look like actual product UI — not a wireframe. Clean data, readable labels, status indicators, the kind of thing that makes someone say "that looks like software I'd actually want to use."
Typography does heavy lifting. Big, confident headline with a gradient on the key phrase. Tight leading. Generous whitespace around it. No paragraph-length headlines.
Motion is purposeful and restrained. One orchestrated hero entrance. Scroll reveals that feel inevitable, not bouncy. Hover states that reward attention without demanding it.
Aviation identity comes through in the content and terminology — not through clipart or cliche imagery. The product IS the aviation identity.

SPOTLIGHT MODULE 1 — Smart Scheduling & Dispatch
The UI panel shows a weekly schedule grid with aircraft rows, colored reservation blocks (lesson green, rental blue, checkride purple, maintenance orange), and a dispatch compliance sidebar showing instructor currency dots and endorsement validity. Copy angle: frictionless booking for members, real-time conflict detection, dispatch that checks compliance automatically — so the front desk isn't the last line of defense.
SPOTLIGHT MODULE 2 — Fleet Intelligence & Maintenance
The UI panel shows an aircraft status board with tail numbers, Hobbs time, next inspection countdowns, open squawk severity indicators, and a utilization trend sparkline per aircraft. Copy angle: three distinct value propositions presented as a tight three-column stat block within the section —
"Turn idle tail numbers into revenue." Flight schools and rental operations have fixed costs that run whether the aircraft flies or not. SkySuite surfaces utilization gaps, flags aircraft sitting idle across booking windows, and gives operators the data to act — turning underperforming assets into scheduled flights.
"Give your club's fixed costs somewhere to land." Flight clubs exist to spread the cost of ownership. SkySuite tracks utilization per member, surfaces who's flying and who isn't, and gives club managers the visibility to keep costs distributed fairly and keep members engaged.
"Operational confidence, not just maintenance logs." SkySuite doesn't replace your A&P or your judgment — it makes sure nothing falls through the cracks. Inspection countdowns, squawk tracking, AD compliance, and airworthiness status are surfaced at dispatch so your team always has the information they need, when they need it. The decisions stay with your people. The visibility stays on.
SPOTLIGHT MODULE 3 — CFI Portal & Compliance
The UI panel shows a student roster with progress bars, endorsement status chips, stage check indicators, and an audit-ready badge. Copy angle: the first platform built for the Chief Instructor, not just the scheduler. Part 61 and Part 141 on enforced separate logic paths. Solo dispatch hard-blocked without a valid current endorsement. Immutable records. One-click FAA Audit Mode. The person whose certificate is on the line finally has the visibility they need.

Stats Bar — Deep navy background. Four large animated counters with gradient numerals. 9 modules · 14 compliance hard gates · 100% records cryptographically hashed · 0 minutes audit prep. Supporting descriptor under each. Subtle radial glow behind numbers.

COPY GUIDANCE FOR FLEET & SAFETY SECTIONS
Never make direct safety claims or imply the software prevents accidents or guarantees compliance. Instead, frame SkySuite as the operational layer that gives your team better information, earlier — so the people responsible for decisions can make them with confidence. Phrases that work: "so nothing falls through the cracks," "the information your team needs, when they need it," "surfaces the gaps before they become problems," "keeps your operation running the way it's supposed to." Phrases to avoid: "prevents accidents," "guarantees compliance," "makes your fleet safer." The tool informs. The people decide.

ANIMATIONS
Hero background: slow-drifting luminous gradient orbs, subtle animated grid lines
Hero entrance: staggered fade-up on badge, headline, subheadline, CTAs, trust line — 80ms delay each
Dashboard mockup: 3D perspective tilt on load, smoothly levels on cursor hover, gentle vertical float loop
Scroll reveals: fade + translateY(24px) on every section, 100-150ms stagger per card
Spotlight module UI panels: slide in from the side as the section enters the viewport
Feature cards: translateY(-6px) lift + gradient border glow on hover
Stats: counter animation from 0 to value, eased, triggered on scroll into view
Utilization sparklines in fleet panel: draw-on animation as the section scrolls into view
CFI progress bars: animate width from 0 on scroll into view
CTA buttons: shimmer sweep on hover (defined in globals.css)
All motion: ease-out curves, nothing bouncy, nothing that calls attention to itself

AUDIENCE & TONE
Flight school owners, Chief Flight Instructors, club managers, dispatchers. They are professionals who know their domain deeply. Write like you respect their intelligence. Short sentences. Active voice. Benefit-driven. Aviation-literate — use correct terminology throughout: tail number, Hobbs/Tach, squawk, CFI/CFII/MEI, VFR/IFR, METAR/TAF, wet/dry rate. Full terminology reference in SKYSUITE_WEBSITE_BRIEF.md Section 6.
The tone is confident and precise — the same way good aviation software should be.
